PPO Bylaw Amendments

At our April 11, 2006 General Meeting of the PPO, changes to the bylaws were presented to the membership in preparation for an amendment vote on May 23, 2006. The PPO bylaws were last significantly revised in January 2000 and, in their current state, fail to reflect changes to the organization that have occurred since then. Our bylaws committee has worked tirelessly to examine and rewrite the bylaws to better serve today's PPO, and has incorporated two revisions ratified on April 11, 2006.

The proposed new bylaws and the current version are both available online for your review.

Scheduling Meeting Recap

On Wednesday, March 1, the PPO hosted a meeting regarding the scheduling changes that had been proposed for next year. The PPO distributed to those in attendance a handout that summarized the rationale for the plan, and questions and concerns related to the implementation. Additionally, PPO minutes of the meeting have been posted in an attempt to keep the BCA community informed.

Mr. Aloia and his district administrative team announced on March 8th that the scheduling changes will not be implemented for the 2006-2007 school year, but that a task force with representatives from the BCA community will continue to explore the possibilities for the following year.

Emergency Closing or Delayed Opening Notification

All students will be notified of any school closings via their Bergen Academy email, in addition to the message posted here at academyppo.com. Parents can also call the Special Announcements line: 201-343-6000 and press #6 for information. There is no formal "phone chain" of phone calls to alert parents of school closing. The flyer for your refrigerator door is available at http://academyppo.com/newsletter/schoolclosingsflier.doc. Note that the response to a weather event may be a full-day closing OR a delayed opening.

AOL Users: Can You Hear Us Now?

Some PPO members are finding the spam-fighting features of recent editions of AOL can get in the way. If you are using AOL 9.0, please add ppomail@academyppo.com to your address book. Otherwise AOL will suspect PPO messages are spam by default.
